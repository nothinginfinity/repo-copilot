# Notion App Store — Business Model

> Captured: 2026-05-09 | Status: Draft | Owner: Jared

## The Core Idea

Own the distribution layer. Jared's apps are *demonstrations* of what the platform enables.
The real product is the infrastructure that lets any developer build, deploy, and sell their own apps through Notion.

This creates three compounding revenue streams:

---

## Three-Tier Product Strategy

### Tier 1 — App Buyer ($10–$100)
> *"I just want a cool app.""

- Lands on the Notion App Store page
- Browses running app demos (Jared's apps are the storefront)
- Buys a single app for ~$10 — gets a Notion page with a Live Site URL + any setup instructions
- Buys a bundle (20 apps) for ~$100 — gets a duplicated Notion database pre-populated with live URLs
- **No GitHub. No code. Just Notion + a link that works.**

### Tier 2 — Developer Seller ($500)
> *"I want to sell my own apps like this."

- Buys the Developer Template
- Gets the full repo-copilot fork + setup guide + sync workflow
- Connects their own GitHub in ~15 minutes
- Their repos auto-populate into their own Notion App Library
- They can now sell apps from their own Notion page — same distribution model
- **Jared earns $500 up front. Developer earns from their own sales.**

### Tier 3 — Platform Cut (future)
> *"I built on your platform and want to reach your audience."

- Developers list their apps on the **main Notion App Store page**
- Jared's store is the primary discovery surface
- Revenue split on sales through the main store (e.g. 70/30 or 80/20)
- **Jared earns passively from every app sold by every developer on the platform.**

## Revenue Model Summary

| Tier | Product | Price | Who buys | Jared's cut |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Single app | ~$10 | End users | 100% |
| 1 | App bundle (20) | ~$100 | End users | 100% |
| 2 | Developer Template | ~$500 | Builders | 100% |
| 3 | Platform listing | Rev share | Developers | 20–30% |

## Distribution Strategy

**Jared's own apps are marketing, not the product.**
Every app Jared ships demonstrates:
- What the platform can do
- The quality bar for apps sold through the store
- Real use cases (games, CRMs, tools, multiplayer experiences)

When someone sees a multiplayer game or a CRM running live inside Notion, they either:
a) Buy the app (Tier 1 revenue)
b) Want to build and sell their own (Tier 2 revenue)

---

## Competitive Moat

Nothing like this exists in the Notion marketplace today:

| What Notion sellers offer today | What this offers |
|---|---|
| Static spreadsheets | Live running applications |
| Checklists + SOPs | Multiplayer games, CRMs, tools |
| One-time templates | Auto-updating, git-versioned apps |
| No developer ecosystem | Full developer distribution layer |

## Phase 3 Build Priorities

1. **Polish the Developer Template** — setup guide, schema doc, workflow (done ✅)
2. **Build 3–5 demo apps** — game, CRM, chat tool, AI tool (these are the storefront)
3. **Landing page** for each tier (Buyer page, Developer page)
4. **Delivery mechanism** — Gumroad or LemonSqueezy for payments, Notion share link for delivery
5. **Developer listing flow** — how a Tier 2 buyer submits their apps to the main store
6. **Platform revenue split** — payment routing for Tier 3 (future, post-MVP)
